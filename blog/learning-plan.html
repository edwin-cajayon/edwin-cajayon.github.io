The Learning Plan (Bootcamp)

Learning Plan (Bootcamp)

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to become a software developer and build a solid career in tech. I want to work on projects that actually help people and keep pushing my skills forward. I’m shifting away from my old industry because I want something that challenges me mentally and gives me more room to grow and create.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

One of my biggest strengths is showing up consistently. I’ve built that habit through training Muay Thai and lifting — and I bring that same energy into learning. I also stay calm under pressure and just keep going.

A limitation I’ve noticed is that I can be too independent. I try to figure everything out on my own, which slows me down sometimes. I’m working on asking for help sooner and not being afraid to speak up when I’m stuck. Also, I tend to get pretty sleepy after work — so staying focused will be a challenge I need to manage carefully.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

My biggest non-technical challenge will be staying focused and energised after a full day of work. I’m still working 6AM to 2:30PM, so jumping straight into study mode afterwards can be tough. I’ll also need to push myself to speak up more in group situations and be okay with not always having the right answer.

What non-technical (human) skills would you like to develop while at Dev Academy?

I’d like to get better at communicating and working in teams. I’m naturally independent, so learning to ask for help and give helpful feedback is something I want to work on. I also want to feel more confident pairing and collaborating with others.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I don’t expect the Bootcamp team to solve problems for me, but I do appreciate support that points me in the right direction when I’m stuck. Honest feedback and encouragement go a long way. I also value a space where it feels safe to be real about what I’m struggling with.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I expect myself to show up every day with a good attitude, even when things get hard. I want to stay open-minded, stay curious, and not be afraid to make mistakes. I also want to support others and be someone that contributes positively to the team.
